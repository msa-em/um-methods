## Assemble Essential Equipment
- Stereo Microscope/ Micromanipulator Setup: Position stereo-microscope with 10-50x magnification capability, ensuring stable mounting and proper illumination. 
- Test all magnification settings and verify alignment. Mount micromanipulator system with fine positioning controls capable of sub-micrometer movements. Verify all axes (X, Y, Z) move smoothly without backlash or stick-slip behavior. Test coarse and fine adjustment knobs for proper response ratios. Ensure mechanical stability under maximum magnification.
- Stage Calibration: Calibrate precise sample mounting stage using reference standards. Verify repeatability of positioning to within ±1 μm. Test stage stability under vibration and thermal conditions. Ensure smooth operation of all mechanical adjustments without drift or play.

### Prepare Heated Quartz Plate System
- Physical Installation: Mount heated quartz plate/removable stage securely on micromanipulator stage using thermally stable clamps. 
- Ensure electrical connections are properly insulated and positioned away from microscope optics. 
- Removable Stage Configuration: Install removable stage component with precise registration features allowing reproducible positioning after removal. Test removal and replacement procedures to ensure sample position repeatability within ±5 μm. Verify visibility for microscope objectives.
- Temperature Control Verification: Connect temperature controller and verify calibration. Test heating rate control and temperature stability (±1°C). Verify temperature uniformity across plate surface.
- Optical Integration: Position heated plate system to maintain optimal working distance for microscope objectives. Verify that heating elements do not interfere with illumination or create optical artifacts. Test visibility and focus quality at all required magnifications during heating cycles.

### Prepare Sample Slides
- Slide Preparation Protocol: Use only high-grade optical glass slides, cleaned with isopropanol, and air dried. Inspect slides under microscope for scratches, particles, or residue that could interfere with observations.
- Sulfur Mounting (Slide #1): Mount high-purity elemental sulfur crystals on clean glass slide using minimal mechanical pressure to avoid contamination. Distribute sulfur evenly to create multiple potential droplet sites. Document sulfur crystal size and distribution with photographs at consistent magnification and lighting.
- Sample Mounting (Slide #2): Mount target astromaterial sample grains on second slide with sufficient spacing to prevent cross-contamination during picking. Use anti-static synthetic sapphire crystal (aluminum oxide, Al₂O₃) brush that has been treated or manufactured to have anti-static properties to prevent particle loss.
- Positioning Optimization: Arrange both slides within micromanipulator stage travel limits, ensuring easy transition between sulfur source and sample areas. Test mechanical movements between positions to verify smooth operation.
Sample Preparation and Documentation

### Initial Sample Documentation
- Comprehensive Photography: Document all sample areas using standardized photography protocols with consistent lighting, magnification, and white balance settings. Capture overview images at low magnification followed by detailed images at higher magnification of each particle cluster. Use calibrated scale bars and include sample identification information in each frame.
- Secure Sample Mounting: Mount meteorite samples on ultra-clean glass slides using minimal force to prevent particle fracturing. Use anti-static mounting techniques to prevent electrostatic particle loss. 
- Detailed Mapping: Create detailed reference maps showing particle positions relative to slide landmarks such as corners or scribed marks. Assign unique identification numbers to each target particle and record size, morphology, and distinguishing characteristics. Use a coordinate measuring system if available for precise position recording.
- Environmental Controls: Record ambient temperature, humidity, and cleanliness conditions. Document any observed particle movement or electrostatic behavior. Note any particles showing signs of alteration, contamination, or mechanical damage that might affect picking success.

## Sulfur Phase Preparation
- Controlled Heating Protocol: Begin heating assembly with precisely controlled ramp rate of 5-10°C per 30 seconds from ambient temperature to 90°C to prevent thermal shock and ensure uniform heating throughout sulfur mass. 
- Temperature Monitoring: Record temperature at regular intervals (every 30-60 seconds) during heating cycle. Watch for any temperature overshoot or oscillation that could indicate control system problems.
- Incremental Temperature Increase: After reaching 90°C, increase temperature in smaller increments of 1-2°C every 2-3 minutes while monitoring sulfur behavior. Continue heating toward target range of 114-116°C, watching for initial signs of phase transition such as crystal boundary softening or surface texture changes.
- Microscopic Monitoring: Maintain continuous observation of sulfur under optical microscopy during entire heating process. Document phase transition progression with time-stamped photographs. Note temperature at which first liquid behavior is observed and when complete melting occurs.

### Confirm Sulfur Melting
- Fluid Behavior Assessment: Verify complete melting by observing characteristic fluid behavior including smooth surface flow, elimination of crystal boundaries, and response to gentle mechanical perturbation with needle tip. Look for complete absence of solid crystalline regions within the molten mass.
- Surface Tension Indicators: Confirm proper surface tension characteristics by observing droplet formation and maintenance of curved meniscus at droplet edges. Test droplet coalescence behavior when adjacent molten regions contact each other. Document optimal working viscosity for particle manipulation.
- Temperature Stabilization: Maintain target temperature of 114-116°C with stability better than ±1°C throughout working period. Verify temperature controller performance and make adjustments as needed. Record actual working temperature for process reproducibility.
- Molten State Documentation: Create comprehensive photographic record of molten sulfur appearance, including color, transparency, and surface characteristics. Document droplet size distribution and spacing on heated plate. Note any signs of thermal decomposition, contamination, or uneven heating.

## Particle Identification and Calibration

### Target Particle Location
- Initial Survey Protocol: Begin systematic survey using lowest magnification to map entire sample area and identify particle clusters of interest. Mark regions of interest for detailed examination.
- Target Selection Criteria: Identify particles based on predetermined selection criteria such as size range (typically 5-50 μm for optimal sectioning), morphology, composition, or specific scientific objectives. Avoid particles showing signs of terrestrial alteration, fracturing, or contamination. Prioritize particles with optimal geometry for intended analysis.
- High-Magnification Examination: Switch to higher magnification for detailed examination of selected particles. Document particle morphology, surface texture, transparency, color, and any internal features visible at high magnification. Record measurements of particle dimensions.
- Priority Ranking System: Establish priority ranking for target particles based on scientific value, picking difficulty, and success probability. Focus initial efforts on highest priority, most accessible particles. Consider creating a backup particle list in case primary targets cannot be successfully picked.

### System Calibration
- Particle Position Recording: Record precise X, Y, Z coordinates for each target particle using either mechanical stage encoders or manual position recording system. Include working distance and magnification settings for each recorded position. Create a lookup table for rapid navigation to each target.
- Sulfur Droplet Mapping: Identify and map optimal sulfur droplet locations considering droplet size, temperature uniformity, accessibility, and optical clarity. Test droplet stability and persistence at working temperature. Save coordinates for multiple backup droplet locations in case primary sites become unsuitable.
- Reference Position Verification: Establish reference positions for glass needle storage, cleaning stations, and emergency parking locations. Test rapid movement between saved positions to ensure smooth operation without collision risk. Create movement protocols for routine position changes.

## Particle Transfer Process

### Approach Target Particles
- Initial Needle Positioning: Begin with a glass needle positioned at safe distance (>1 mm) above sample surface using coarse positioning controls. Verify needle tip is clean and undamaged under high magnification. Switch to fine positioning mode and begin controlled descent at a rate of 1-2 μm per adjustment.
- Focus Management: Maintain sharp focus on needle tip throughout approach by adjusting microscope focus as needed. Use parfocal objectives when changing magnification to minimize refocusing time. Monitor both needle tip position and target particle simultaneously using optimal depth of field settings.
- Vibration Control: Eliminate building vibrations, air currents, and mechanical disturbances during approach phase. Make movements only during stable conditions, pausing approach if any disturbance is detected. Allow the system to settle between movements.
- Approach Angle Optimization: Position needle to approach particle at optimal angle (typically 15-30° from horizontal) to facilitate particle adhesion while minimizing risk of particle displacement. 
- Final Positioning: Reduce approach rate to minimum controllable increment (typically 0.1-0.5 μm steps) when needle tip reaches 10-20 μm from target particle. Monitor particles and substrate for any sign of electrostatic interaction or mechanical disturbance that might affect picking success.

### Gentle Particle Contact
- Contact Force Control: Make initial contact with minimal force, just sufficient to create physical contact between needle tip and particle surface. Avoid pressing particles against substrates which could cause particle fracturing or substrate contamination. Monitor contact through microscope at highest practical magnification.
- Magnification Consistency: Maintain consistent magnification throughout contact and lifting process to preserve spatial reference and depth perception. Use magnification that provides optimal compromise between field of view and resolution. Avoid changing magnification during critical manipulation phases.
- Adhesion Assessment: Test particle adhesion to needle tip by making gentle lifting motion (1-2 μm vertical displacement) while observing particle response. If the particle does not adhere, try a different contact point, clean needle tip, or adjust approach angle. Some particles may require multiple contact attempts.
- Lifting Technique: Once adhesion is confirmed, lift particle/s slowly and steadily using controlled vertical movement. Maintain a constant lifting rate to avoid sudden acceleration that could break adhesion or damage particles. Monitor for any sign of particle rotation or deformation during the lifting process.

### Transfer to Molten Sulfur
- Navigation Protocol: Move particle-loaded needle to predetermined sulfur droplet location using saved coordinates and established movement protocols. Use smooth, continuous motion to minimize particle loss due to vibration or acceleration. Monitor particle attachment throughout transit.
- Thermal Considerations: Account for thermal effects when moving from ambient temperature sample area to heated sulfur region. Allow a brief equilibration period if needed to prevent thermal shock to particles or sudden adhesion changes due to needle thermal expansion.
Sulfur Droplet Assessment: Verify sulfur droplet remains in optimal molten state with proper temperature and viscosity. Check for contamination, crystallization, or thermal decomposition that might affect embedding quality. Select alternative droplet location if the primary site is compromised.

:::{tip}
:class: myclass1 myclass2
:name: a-tip-reference
Initial sulfur melt (around 115 °C) should melt into a pale yellow, low-viscosity liquid. If sulfur becomes orange, heat is too high (>116 °C or on heat too long), it will polymerize and structurally change.
:::

- Controlled Descent: Lower particle-loaded needle into molten sulfur medium using controlled descent rate (typically 2-5 μm/second) while monitoring particle behavior and sulfur response. Watch for any bubble formation that could displace particles.
- Electrostatic Positioning: Employ electrostatic positioning techniques by adjusting needle potential or using grounded probes to achieve precise particle placement within sulfur droplets. Use minimal electrical fields to avoid particle damage while providing sufficient force for positioning control.
- Surface Penetration: Ensure specimen penetrates sulfur surface and becomes fully immersed below surface level. Monitor surface tension effects and adjust technique as needed to overcome surface resistance. Document particle depth and orientation relative to droplet surface.

## Particle Positioning and Embedding

### Optimize Particle Placement
- Lateral Positioning Control: Execute lateral movements within sulfur droplet using fine control settings to center particle within droplet volume. Move particle incrementally (1.0 μm steps) while monitoring position relative to droplet boundaries. Account for sulfur viscosity effects on movement response time.
- Depth Optimization: Push particle deeper below the sulfur surface to achieve optimal embedding depth, typically 2-3 particle diameters from final surface. Monitor particle orientation during depth adjustment to maintain optimal sectioning plane alignment. Use gentle, steady pressure to avoid particle damage.
- Three-Dimensional Positioning: Consider particle position in all three spatial dimensions to ensure optimal embedding geometry. Adjust X-Y position for centering while simultaneously controlling Z-depth for proper burial. 
Matrix Thickness Control: Ensure uniform sulfur thickness on all sides of the particle by maintaining central positioning within the droplet. Monitor minimum sulfur thickness (should be >5 μm on all sides) to provide adequate mechanical support during sectioning. Adjust particle position or add additional molten sulfur if needed.

### Achieve Optimal Embedding Configuration
- Uniform Distribution Assessment: Verify uniform sulfur distribution around particles. Look for complete sulfur coverage without voids, air pockets, or areas of insufficient thickness that could compromise mechanical integrity.
- Air Bubble Prevention: Minimize air bubble incorporation through controlled movement techniques and proper sulfur temperature maintenance. If bubbles are observed, use needle tip to guide bubbles away from particle vicinity or burst surface bubbles before crystallization begins.
- Particle Integrity Check: Verify particles remain intact and undamaged throughout the positioning process. Look for signs of fracturing, surface alteration, or contamination that might have occurred during manipulation. If damage is observed, consider repositioning or selecting alternative/additional particles.
- Final Position Documentation: Create comprehensive photographic record of final particle position including multiple viewing angles and magnifications. Record precise coordinates and orientation data. Document sulfur droplet geometry and any special conditions or observations.

## Controlled Crystallization Process

### Initiate Controlled Nucleation
- Nucleation Point Introduction Methods: Introduce nucleation points through carefully controlled mechanical agitation using clean needle tungsten tip to ensure uniform crystallization.
- Timing Optimization: Time nucleation introduction precisely to avoid disrupting final particle positioning while ensuring adequate control over crystal growth process. Initiate nucleation when sulfur temperature has stabilized and particle position is confirmed optimal. Allow 30-60 seconds after final positioning before beginning nucleation.
- Nucleation Site: Apply minimal force (just sufficient to create local disturbance in sulfur) at predetermined locations around particle periphery. Ensure nucleation sites are distributed to promote uniform crystallization progression toward particle center. Avoid creating a nucleation site too close to the particle (within 10-20 μm) that might cause rapid localized crystallization and particle displacement.

### Manage Crystallization Process
- Controlled Cooling Protocol: Implement gradual cooling at a rate of 10°C per minute while maintaining nucleation point activity. Monitor temperature reduction carefully to prevent thermal shock that could cause rapid crystallization and internal stress development.
- Crystal Growth Monitoring: Observe crystal growth progression under microscope using crossed polarizers if available to enhance crystal visibility. Look for characteristic sulfur crystal morphology and growth patterns. 
- Fine-Grained Structure Promotion: Maintain conditions that promote fine-grained, uniform crystal structure by controlling cooling rate and nucleation density. Avoid rapid temperature changes that could cause large crystal formation or preferential growth directions that might compromise mechanical properties.
- Stress Concentration Prevention: Monitor for formation of large, irregular crystals that create stress concentrations and preferential cleavage planes. If large crystals begin forming, increase cooling rate slightly or introduce additional nucleation points to interrupt the growth process.

### Complete Solidification
- Final Crystallization Monitoring: Allow complete crystallization while maintaining continuous observation of particle positioning stability. Watch for any particle movement or rotation that might occur during the final solidification phase. Document any position changes for quality control assessment.
- Matrix Integrity Verification: Verify formation of solid crystalline structure throughout the entire sulfur matrix for absence of soft spots, voids, or incomplete crystallization that could affect sectioning quality. Systematically inspect the entire embedded sample for presence of voids, cracks, or inclusions that could compromise sectioning integrity. Use transmitted and reflected light illumination to detect subsurface defects. Document location and extent of any observed defects.
- Thermal Equilibration: Allow embedded sample to reach ambient temperature gradually (typically 2-3°C per minute cooling rate) to minimize thermal stress and prevent crack formation. Monitor for any crack development during the cooling process and document any observed thermal stress effects. 


## Quality Control and Documentation

### Final Verification
Comprehensive Photography Protocol: 
- Execute systematic photographic documentation using standardized imaging protocols.
- Capture images at multiple magnifications and lighting conditions (transmitted, reflected) to fully characterize embedded sample. 
- Use consistent white balance, exposure, and focus settings for all documentation.

### Before/After Comparison Analysis
- Compare final embedded particle images with initial documentation to verify particle identity and assess any changes in morphology, orientation, or integrity that occurred during processing.
- Create side-by-side comparison images with identical magnification and orientation for quality assessment.
- Particle Identity Confirmation: Verify embedded particle matches original target particle through detailed morphological comparison including size measurements, surface features, color, and any distinctive characteristics. 
- Embedding Quality Assessment: Evaluate embedding quality by examining sulfur matrix characteristics including crystal size distribution, optical clarity, mechanical integrity, and uniformity of sulfur distribution around particle. Document any defects, voids, or areas of concern that might affect subsequent processing.
- Position Accuracy Documentation: Record final particle position within sulfur matrix including depth below surface, centering accuracy, and orientation relative to intended sectioning plane. Calculate deviations from target position.


### Storage and Preparation for Sectioning
- Controlled Temperature Equilibration: Allow embedded samples to reach ambient temperature through controlled cooling process taking 60 minutes to overnight. Monitor temperature reduction rate to stay within 1-2°C per minute to prevent thermal shock and crack formation.

- Environmental Storage Controls: Store embedded samples in a controlled environment with stable temperature (20±2°C), low humidity (<50% RH), and minimize mechanical stress. Use desiccant if needed to control moisture exposure.
- Sample Labeling Protocol: Apply comprehensive labeling system including unique sample identification numbers, preparation date, operator identification, and critical preparation parameters. Use permanent marking methods resistant to solvents and mechanical handling during subsequent processing.
- Quality Pre-Assessment: Conduct final quality assessment before releasing samples for sectioning including visual inspection, dimensional verification, and mechanical integrity testing. Document sample condition and any recommendations or precautions for sectioning personnel.
