## Results

:::{figure} ./figures/Fig4_mm.jpg
:name: final_example_tem_sample
Dark field STEM image of a ~ 50 nm microtomed section of a macromolecular polymer after sulfur sublimation. The porous polymer section is suspended over a hole in a carbon film, suitable for background-free nanoscale imaging and spectroscopy.
:::

Ultramicrotomy of sulfur-embedded samples yielded high-quality ribbons with minimal sectioning artifacts. The optimal particle size range for this protocol was determined to be 10-15 μm diameter, with larger particles (>15 μm) exhibiting increased susceptibility to plucking artifacts after 10-15 successful sections. Particle plucking occurred when mechanical stress concentrated at the boundaries between particles and the surrounding matrix material. This problem was most severe in areas containing hard mineral particles that had very different stiffness properties compared to the matrix. The plucking happened when the cutting process reached the widest part of each particle, indicating that the bond between the particle and matrix wasn't strong enough to withstand the cutting forces at that point. Quality assessment revealed that a majority of sections met criteria for TEM analysis (uniform thickness, minimal compression, minimal chatter), with 20-25 acceptable sections obtainable per particle before plucking onset.
