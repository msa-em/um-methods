Figure 1. Components of an ultramicrotome for electron microscopy sample preparation. The ultramicrotome consists of key components including the arm/sample chuck which moves the embedded sample block toward the diamond or glass knife mounted in the knife holder. The knife boat (attached to diamond knife) has a water bath that contains ultrapure water to collect thin sections as they are cut. Progress is seen through a stereomicroscope that provides magnified visualization of the sectioning process, allowing the operator to monitor section quality and manipulate ribbons. The microtome stage advance mechanism controls the feed rate to produce sections suitable for transmission electron microscopy. The instrument base is mounted on an anti-vibration table to minimize chatter artifacts during sectioning.
